Overview

Memory and data capacities double approximately every two years and, apparently, the Web is following the same rule. User-generated contents, in particular, are an ever-growing source of opinion and sentiments which are continuously spread worldwide through blogs, wikis, fora, chats and social networks. The distillation of knowledge from such sources is a key factor for applications in fields such as commerce, tourism, education and health, but the quantity and the nature of the contents they generate make it a very difficult task. Due to such challenging research problems and wide variety of practical applications, opinion mining and sentiment analysis have become very active research areas in the last decade.

Our understanding and knowledge of the problem and its solution are still limited as natural language understanding techniques are still pretty weak. Most of current research in sentiment analysis, in fact, merely relies on machine learning algorithms. Such algorithms, despite most of them being very effective, produce no human understandable results such that we know little about how and why output values are obtained. All such approaches, moreover, rely on syntactical structure of text, which is far from the way the human mind processes natural language. Next-generation opinion mining systems should employ techniques capable to better grasp the conceptual rules that govern sentiment and the clues that can convey these concepts from realization to verbalization in the human mind.

Scope / Topics

  • Foundation models for sentiment and emotion analysis.
  • Explainable and trustworthy sentiment analysis.
  • Emotion and sentiment recognition in multimodal content (text, audio, video, image).
  • Multilingual and cross-lingual sentiment analysis.
  • Dynamic and temporal modeling of affective states.
  • Context-aware and personalized sentiment interpretation.
  • Aspect-based sentiment analysis and opinion role labeling.
  • Sarcasm, irony, and humor detection.
  • Commonsense reasoning for affective understanding.
  • Large-scale affective knowledge graphs and representation learning.
  • Multimodal fusion strategies for affective computing.
  • Low-resource and zero-shot approaches for opinion mining.
  • Bias, fairness, and ethics in sentiment and opinion analysis.
  • Affect-aware human-computer interaction and conversational AI.
  • Fake news, misinformation, and opinion manipulation detection.
  • Emotionally intelligent virtual agents and chatbots.
  • Domain and culture adaptation for affective computing.
  • Emotion-aware recommender systems and search.
  • Applications in marketing, mental health, education, and entertainment.

Submission

Paper Submission: Submissions are invited to the ICDM 2026 Workshop on SENTIRE, to be held in conjunction with IEEE ICDM 2026 in Shenyang, China.
Paper Format: Authors must follow the IEEE Proceedings Author Guidelines. The paper length is limited to 10 pages including references, figures, and appendices. The camera-ready version is limited to 8 pages, with up to 2 additional pages allowed at extra cost.
Review Process: Each submission will be reviewed by at least three Program Committee members based on originality, significance, technical quality, and clarity.
Publication: Accepted papers will be included in the IEEE ICDM Workshop Proceedings published by IEEE Computer Society Press, and will be indexed by IEEE Xplore, Scopus, and Web of Science.
Submission Policy: Submitted papers must not be under review or accepted elsewhere. Extended versions of selected papers may be invited to special issues in relevant journals.
